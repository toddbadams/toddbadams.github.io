Tuscany

Located on the western coast of central Italy

Local Varieties

Sangiovese high acid high tannins red cherries, plums and dried herbs, meaty and gamey with age usually oaked

Chainti DOCG

Located in the north of Tuscany in the mountains., at the base of the Apennines. Divided into seven sub-zones. Chianti DOCG is the greater region, Chiantie Rufina DOCG and Chianti Colli Senesi DOCG are well known sub regions. Chianti Classico DOCG is not a sub-zone, but are original vineyar area. Higher altitude, which slows the ripening, resulting in greater acidity and more herbal aromas.

Local Varieties

Sangiovese blended with small amounts of other black grapes (Italiant and French)

Chainti Classico DOCG

Chianti Classico DOCG is not a sub-zone, but are original vineyar area. Higher altitude, which slows the ripening, resulting in greater acidity and more herbal aromas. Aged for 12 Months.

Chainti Classico Riserva DOCG

Aged for 24 Months, with at least 3 months in bottle. Gran Selezione is highest designation, grapes must be source from single estated, aged for six months longer than Riserva. No requirements converning oak ageing, however vast majority is matured in oak for part of the aging process.

Southern Tuscany

at lower altitudes than the north, therefore warmer climate. Cool maritime breeze from south west moderates temperature. Brunello di Montelcino DOCG (sangiovese min ageing of 5 years, 2 in oak) and Vino Nobile di Montepulciano DOCG are best known appelations in southern Tuscany. Due to climate, wines are richer and fuller-bodied than Chianti. Rosso di Montoalcino DOC and Rosso di Montepulciano DOC are declassified versions of Brunello di Montelcino DOCG and Vino Nobile di Montepulciano DOCG.

The Coast

Cooled by sea breezes, flat coastal region.

Local Varieties

Black Bordeaux varieties and other international grapes widely grown. Toscana IGT from this area with international grapes, recently Bolgheri DOC and Maremma Toscann DOC permite wines from non-Italian varieties (cab sav, merlot, syrah)

Umbria

Mediterranean contenential climate White wines Orvieto DOC, a blend of Grechetto, Trbbiano di Toscana, and other local grapes. Light in body with medium to high acidity, flavours of ripe grapefruit and peaches. Cool fermentation in stainless steel. Reg wine Sagrantino di Montefalco DOCG, thick-skinned Sagrantino grape, medium to high acid, high tannin, high alcohol, flavours of black cherries and plums. Some oak and need considerable time in bottle.
