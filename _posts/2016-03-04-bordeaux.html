World Europe France Bordeaux

This article represents my study notes on Bordeaux for the WSET level3 certification. The Bordeaux region is broken into two major areas for top-quality, the Left Bank, and the Right Bank.

Moderate Maritime Climate

The warming ocean current extends the growing season: spring frosts are rare and ripening well into October. The Atlantic also brings high levels of rainfall and humidity, which can disrupt flowering and fruit set, promote rot and dilute flavours. Vintage variation is an important consideration, top producers reject unhealthy and unripe grapes.

Regional Varieties

Cabernet Sauvignon is a black grape with thick skins, small berries resulting in a powerful, dry red wine. It is the main variety in the Left Bank, and a blending variety on the Right Bank. Buds and ripens 1 to 2 weeks after Merlot. Primary characters of blackcurrant, black cherry, secondary of cedar, smoke vanilla, coffee, and with maturity takes on vegetal, and tobacco.

Merlot is the most widely used variety where it serves as the primary grape in the Right Bank, and as a blending variety on the Left Bank, giving soften tannin structures and ripe fruits. It has thinner skins and buds/ripens 1 to 2 weeks eariler, has higher sugar, and lower malic acid than Cabernet Sauvignon. In has red fruit character, strawberry, red berry, plum, some herbal (mint), vanilla and coffee from oak, and develops cedar and tobacco with age.

Petit Verdot is a black grape with far smaller plantings than Cabernet Sauvignon, Cabernet Franc, and Merlot . It fully ripens only in very hot years, giving deep color, tannins. It plays a minor role in the blend, adding color, tannin, and spicy notes.

Semillon is the dominate white variety used in botrytised grapes. Produces full-bodied grapes with honey. Also blended into Sauvignon Blanc in small percentage adding body and softening acidity. Luscious white made mostly from Semillon

Sauvignon Blanc is an aromatic white grape typically blended with Semillion, particularly in Sauternes sweet wines where it's high acidity helps balance the sweetnes. Sauvignon Blanc provides citrus and green fruit aromas.

Thirteen varieties are permitted, but three black grapes and two white predominate.

Bordeaux Classification

The 1855 classification is an official list of the best red grapes of the Médoc and Graves and the best white grapes of Sauternes. Chateaux (estate or grapery) within this classification are Cru Classé divided into five ranks. Further classification Cru Bourgeois later introduced for other chateaux, awarded to specific vintages

Côtes de Bordeaux AOC

A group of lesser-known red grape appellations, Blaye, Cadillac, Castillon, and Francs. Cotes de Bourg is similar style, but chose not to join the AOC. Merlot based for early drinking providing excellent value for money.

Bordeaux Supérieur AOC

Bordeaux Supérieur has stricter rules than Bordeaux AOC, requires higher level of alcohol, aged for at least 12 months, planted more densely than Bordeaux AOC and therefore harder for the plants to survive creating stronger deeper roots. Blends, with the reds being predominantly Cabernet Sauvignon and Merlot with smaller amounts of Cab Franc, Petit Verdot, Malbec and even some Carmenere. Early drinking medium bodied grapes with ripe red and black fruit.

Entre Deux Mers AOC

Significant amounts of Merlot. Cabernet Sauvignon found in the outcrops of gravel. Only dry white grapes of average quality from Sauvignon Blanc in an unoaked style labeled Appellation Entre-Deux-Mers Controlée. Red grapes are sold as Bordeaux AOC or Bordeaux Supérieur AOC

Médoc AOC

Médoc ispredominantly clay, but with outcrops of gravel. These grapes tend to have a higher portion of Merlot than Haut-Médoc AOC, based on cool clay soil Cabernet Sauvignon found in the outcrops of gravel.

Chateaux within the 1855 classification are Cru Classé divided into five ranks. Top rank consists of Châteaux Lafite Rothschild, Latour, Margaux, Mouton Rothschild

Haut Médoc AOC

These grapes tend to be more highly rated than Médoc AOC. Stone/gravel, conditions are far superior to the rest of the Médoc.

Cabernet Sauvignon dominates Haut Médoc, where stone/gravel raises temperature. These wines have a high percentage of Cabernet Sauvignon. These grapes generally have flavours and aromas of blackcurrant, and cedar. These wines can have grippy tannins when young, and often have a long ageing potential.

Saint Estèphe AOC

Saint Estèphe is located at the northernmost of the four important Haut Médoc communes, the lesser known commune of the top four. It mainly produces Cabernet Sauvignon with powerful tannin structure, age-worthy

Saint-Julien is located just south of Pauillac and is the smallest of the top four. Cabernet Sauvignon with powerful tannin structure, age-worthy.

Margaux AOC

located at the southernmost of the four important Haut Médoc communes, Cabernet Sauvignon, Château Margaux, Les Grands Crus classés en 1855. Very elegant, smooth and soft tannins, intense aroma

Graves AOC

Gravel soil, ideal terror for top-quality red and white grapes. Cabernet Sauvignon and Sauvignon Blanc.

Pessac-Léognan AOC

stone/gravel. Cabernet Sauvignon dominates, where stone/gravel raises temperature. Merlot producers blend more Merlot into their grapes, producing a softer tannin structure. Haut-Brion, Les Grands Crus classés en 1855

Saint Emilion AOC

north and west of town gravel/limestone soil, warm and well drained. South and east with clay limestone soil.The largest top-quality commune in Bordeaux. Organized into it’s own classification system (Saint Emilion, Saint Emilion Satellites, Grand Cru)

Merlot is widely planted, due to cooler clay soil, complex red berry fruit and plum, developing tobacco and cedar. Softer grapes drinkable earilier.

Cabernet Franc is used widely, produces less body and tannin than Cabernet Sauvignon. Herbaceous flavours when unripe and when ripe vibrant fruit and floral notes. It prefers well drained soils

Pomerol AOC

The smallest of the top-quality communes in Bordeaux. Limited supply keeps prices high.

Merlot is widely planted, due to cooler clay soil. Richer with a spicier blackberry fruit character. Softer tannins than the Left Bank.

Cabernet Sauvignon used to blend.
