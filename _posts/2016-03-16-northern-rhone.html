Steep slopes with south-facing aspects, These AOC wines are exclusively Syrah with deep colour, medium to high tannins, black fruit, and sometimes black pepper and floral aromas.

The valley is narrow and steep, and vineyards are mainly planted close to the river, where the best have south-facing aspect. Much smaller region than Southern Rhône

Moderate Continental Climate

Temperatures are noticeably cooler than in the Southern Rhône. The sites are often in lateral valleys which help protect from the cold north winds known as the mistral, which flows down the Rhône from the Massif Central.

Varieties

Syrah black

Syrah is the only black variety permitted, and is at the northern limit of successful ripening, therefore southern aspect and steep slopes are important. Deep color, medium to high tanning, black fruit, sometimes black pepper and floral aromas. Some winemakers use high portion of new oak, others use older oak or larger barrels.

Viognier white

Viognier is full-bodied white with perfumed aromas of blossom and apricots, flavours of stone fruits. Sometimes blended with Syrah to add aromatic intensity. Very late ripening, so the wines tend to have low acidity and high alcohol. New oak can be used, but can overwhelm the varietal aromas. Some late-harvested off-dry styles.

Marsanne and Roussanne white

Marsanne and Roussanne usually blended together. Marsanne has richness and weight, Roussanne offers acidity and perfumed fruit characteristics. These wines do not have aromatic intensity of Viognier but age with complex hazelnut aromas.

Côte Rôtie (roasted slope) AOC

The most northern appellation, almost entirely steep slopes with southern aspects.

Varieties

Syrah black

Only red wines are produced, up to 20 percent Viognier can be blended, but in practice only a limited number of wines produced with Viognier in the blend, and then only a small percentage. Deep color, full-bodied, spicy, and the best have aromatic, floral freshness, intense flavors, complexity, and textural elegance.

Condrieu AOC

Just south of Côte Rôtie . Steep, well-exposed, terraced vineyards.

Varieties

Viognier white

A white wine made from 100% Viognier, the best from low-yield old vines.

Saint-Joseph AOC

Varieties

Syrah black

The best from terraced vineyards, which have intensity and structure similar to Hermitage. Flatter areas on valley floor use carbonic maceration and have lightest body of Northern Rhône, with pepper perfumed aromas. Blended with up to 10% Marsanne and Roussanne

Hermitage AOC

Steep south-facing slopes that vary in steepness and aspect. The best wines are blended from different sites within the AOC.

Varieties

Syrah black

These wines are the fullest bodied of Northern Rhône and ages well for decades. Up to 15% of Marsanne and Roussanne can be fermented with, but rarely done, adds complexity.

Marsanne and Roussanne white

Marsanne and Roussanne usually blended together, and account for 20% of Hermitage production.

Crozes Hermitage AOC

Mix terrain located around the hill of Hermitage.

Varieties

Syrah black

Up to 15% of Marsanne and Roussanne can be fermented with, but rarely done. Quality, style, and price varies depending on location of the vineyard. Lighter wines are from high yielding, flatter vineyards. More concentrated, complex, and tannic wines from steeper slopes.

Cornas AOC

Southernmost location, and by far the warmest. Sheltered, well-exposed sun-baked south-facing slopes.

Varieties

Syrah black

100% Syrah in Cornas, deep color, full-bodied, style and quality similar to Hermitage
